Guitar

Electric Guitar

Electric guitars generate sound through magnetic pickups that convert string vibrations into electrical signals, which are then sent to an amplifier. They usually have solid bodies and don't rely on acoustic resonance, which makes them quieter on their own but highly expressive when amplified. Electric guitars are widely used in genres such as rock, blues, metal, jazz, and funk.

Acoustic Guitar

Acoustic guitars are self-contained instruments that produce sound through the natural vibration of strings amplified by a hollow wooden body. They require no external equipment, making them convenient and portable for casual play, outdoor use, or unplugged performances. Acoustic guitars are commonly used in genres like folk, country, pop, and classical music.

Bass Guitar

The bass guitar is a stringed instrument similar in appearance to an electric guitar but with a longer neck and lower pitch, typically used to provide rhythm, depth, and harmonic foundation in music across many genres.

Bass Guitar Parts

The Neck: the part that holds the fretboard, frets and headstock.
The Headstock: the part that holds the tuning pegs and anchors the strings.
The Body: the part that holds pickups, bridge, and controls.
The Bridge: anchor point for strings and transfers vibrations to the instrument’s body.

Drums

Drums are percussion instruments played by striking them, often with sticks or hands. Found in many cultures, they provide rhythm and energy in music. Modern drum kits include bass drums, snares, toms, and cymbals, and are used in genres like rock, jazz, and pop.
